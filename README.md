# EY-Data-Challenge-2026

In the 2026 EY AI & Data Challenge, my teammate Nathan Mak @HowaiMak and I developed a machine learning pipeline to predict critical water quality parameters: Total Alkalinity, Electrical Conductivity, and Dissolved Reactive Phosphorus, for rivers across South Africa. The core hurdle was spatial out-of-distribution (OOD) prediction, which required us to forecast water quality in a geographic region completely absent from our training data. To tackle this, we systematically identified and removed "spatial proxies" that falsely encoded geographic locations, focusing instead on engineering genuine local-scale physical features. We then designed a novel two-stage GAM-Residual Ensemble Boosting architecture, combining a Generalised Additive Model to capture interpretable macro-scale trends with a three-way tree ensemble to model complex, non-linear micro-scale residuals.
